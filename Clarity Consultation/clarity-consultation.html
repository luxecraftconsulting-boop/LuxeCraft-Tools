Step 1 of 7
01
Welcome & Set the Frame
5 minutes
Warm them up, establish the agenda, and make them feel like this is a high-value use of their time — not a sales pitch.
Your opening script
  • Thank them for making time. "I know you're running a business — I don't take this lightly."
  • Set the frame: "This isn't a pitch — it's a conversation. I'm going to ask you some real questions about how your business runs, and we'll figure out together if what I do is a fit."
  • Let them know the format: "We'll spend most of our time on your world. I'll save a few minutes at the end to share how I typically work."
  • Confirm time: "You've got about [X] minutes? Perfect — let's dig in."
Opening question
"Before we get into anything — what made you say yes to this call today?"
First impression
How did they show up? Energy, prepared, skeptical, eager?
02
Their Business
10 minutes
Build rapport and get a clear picture of who they are, what they build, and where they operate. Listen more than you talk.
The business
"Tell me a little about your business — what do you do?"
Location
"Where are you located? Are you working locally, regionally, or broader?"
Team size
"What is the size of your team right now?"
What they build & for who
"What do you build, and who do you build it for?"
Size & scale
"How many projects are you running at once? What's the typical project size?"
Contract type
"How do you structure your contracts — cost plus, fixed price, time and materials, or a mix?"
03
JobTread Usage
10 minutes
Understand where they are in JobTread — how long they've been in it, what they're actually using, and where the gaps are. This shapes everything you recommend.
How long in JobTread
"How long have you been using JobTread?"
What they're using
"At a high level, what pieces of JobTread are you actually using right now?"
Major gaps & inefficiencies
"Where are your major gaps or inefficiencies inside JobTread? What's not working the way you need it to?"
04
Pain & Goals
10 minutes
This is the core of the call. Ask the real questions. Let them vent. Listen for the gap between where they are and where they want to be.
The big pain
"What's your biggest pain right now — inside or outside of JobTread?"
Where things fall apart
"Where do things tend to fall apart? Field, office, handoffs between the two?"
Team buy-in
"Is your team actually using JobTread consistently, or is it more you than them?"
Their ideal state
"If your operations were running exactly the way you want — what does that actually look like on a Tuesday?"
Cost of inaction
"What happens if the next 12 months look a lot like the last 12?"
05
Current State Assessment
8 minutes
Get tactical on their setup, their tech stack, and what they've tried before. This tells you the complexity of the engagement.
How they got set up
"How did you get set up in JobTread — did you figure it out yourself, or did someone help configure it?"
What's working well
"What parts of your operation — inside or outside JobTread — are actually working the way you want?"
Outside systems & tech stack
"What else are you running alongside JobTread? Walk me through your current tech stack."
Previous attempts to fix this
"Have you tried to solve this before — hired someone, taken a course, built your own system, tried another platform?"
06
What I Do & How I Work
8 minutes
Now you talk. Connect everything they told you back to how you solve it. This is a bridge, not a brochure.
How to frame this
  • Start by reflecting: "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing..." — mirror their language, not yours.
  • Then: "This is exactly the kind of situation I work with. Let me tell you how I approach it."
  • Intro Phase 0: "I always start with a Discovery & Review — I'm not going to build your system without knowing exactly what's there."
  • Walk through Phase 1 (System Build) and Phase 2 (Legacy Builder) at a high level — outcomes, not features.
  • If relevant: mention the Support Lab as an ongoing resource for the team.
  • Anchor on the result: "My clients stop running on chaos. They run like the premium operation they're capable of being."
Notes from this conversation
What came up as you walked them through what you do? What resonated, what questions did they ask?
Fit check
"Does this feel like what you've been looking for?"
Questions they raise
What do they ask about specifically?
07
The Close
5 minutes
Clear, confident, no hedging. You know if this is a fit. Ask for the next step directly.
Closing language
  • Summarize what you heard: "You told me [pain point]. That's fixable. Here's what I'd recommend."
  • Name the engagement: "I'd like to start with a Phase 0 Discovery & Review. At the end you'll have a complete picture of where your system stands and a roadmap for what to build."
  • Ask directly: "Does that feel like the right place to start?"
  • If they hesitate: "What would you need to feel confident moving forward?"
  • If it's not a fit: "I want to be straight with you — I don't think this is the right time. Here's why, and here's what I'd do instead."
Recommended engagement
Which service is the right fit based on what you heard?
Their decision
How did they respond to the close?
Objections raised

Agreed next step
