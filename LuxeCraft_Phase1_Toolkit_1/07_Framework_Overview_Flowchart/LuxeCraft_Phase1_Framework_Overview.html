REPEATABLE PATTERN

The Rollout Playbook

Every team rollout, regardless of which week or which tool, follows the same four-step pattern. The team learns the rhythm, which makes change predictable.

STEP 1
Pre-Approval
One week before. Slide deck and Loom sent to leadership and Champion. Approval required on readiness and accuracy before kickoff.
STEP 2
Team Kickoff
Day 1 of rollout week. Live Zoom with full team. Champion co-leads. Recorded for absent and reference.
STEP 3
Practice Week
Days 2 to 5. Team uses the tool in real work. Champion is first point of contact. Consultant monitors adoption remotely.
STEP 4
Adoption Check
End of rollout week. Champion and consultant review who is using it, who is not, what is breaking. Refine before next week.

WEEKLY 30-MIN CALL

Champion Session Structure

Every Champion call follows the same seven-element structure. Back-to-back with the 1-hour team call, same day.

1
Accountability Check
What they committed to last week and how it went
2
Review the Playbook Chapter
What landed, what did not, questions surfaced
3
Reflection Questions
Listen to what is happening on the team
4
Talking Points
Leadership coaching content for the week
5
Watch-Fors
Specific behaviors to notice in the team
6
Self-Sufficiency Drill
Scenario or question they work through alone
7
Capture the Commitment
What they will commit to this week
PRINCIPLE 01

Lowest Friction First

Team rollout starts with tools that have no dependencies and the lowest cognitive load. Habit-building first. Heavy tools layer on once the daily JobTread habit is real. Sequence is dependency-driven, not friction-driven.

PRINCIPLE 02

Champion-Led, Not Consultant-Led

The consultant is temporary. The Champion is permanent. Every team rollout is co-led by the Champion. By Week 12, the Champion runs the system. We have built a leader, not just configured a tool.

PRINCIPLE 03

Repeatable Playbook, Every Time

Every team rollout follows the same 4-step pattern. Every Champion call follows the same 7 elements. Every engagement starts with the same scorecard. Repetition is the point. Predictability is what makes adoption stick.
